ELECTED POSITIONS:

Senior Patrol Leader

  • THOMAS LIGGETT: I'm a Life Scout and have been actively participating in Troop 109 ever since I finished Cub Scouts. I have been Patrol Leader for the No Name Patrol, Assistant Patrol Leader for the Bear Patrol, as well as Quartermaster. I believe I would be a good Senior Patrol Leader because I value communication and leading by example.
  • DANIEL KOPISCH: I decided to run for Senior Patrol Leader (SPL). Here are the top five reasons why you should vote for me.
    5) Experience. I have served three terms as Assistant Senior Patrol Leader (ASPL) and learned which obligations an SPL has.
    4) Training. I was the first scout (with my brother) in Troop 109 to attend the National Youth Leadership Training (NYLT) to learn how to lead in a Boy Scout troop.
    3) Insight. I have served Troop 109 in many roles (currently I'm Troop Guide) and understand the different requirements you have.
    2) Focus. In order to see an Eagle Scout project through a Life Scout needs to put his complete focus on it. I'm basically done with my Eagle Scout project and can fully focus on leading Troop 109.
    1) Guts. I have proven that I'm not afraid to speak up and stand my ground in the best interest of the scouts. Our troop should be boy led. I was the only scout who wrote an email to request a discussion of the adult decision to assign scouts to leadership positions. I also was the only scout who spoke up during the troop meeting to ensure that we govern ouselves.
    So if you want someone who can stand his ground on your behalf and knows how to do the job of an SPL then you should vote for me.

Service Coordinator

  • JOE FARNED: I'm interested in serving as your Troop Service Coordinator for the upcoming Scouting season. In the past, Troop 109 has not been very "service oriented". At most, we've participated in two or three "smaller" projects per year. As Service Coordinator, I would strive to substantially increase our involvement in service projects. Preferably, the Troop would participate in a service project – each likely lasting for a few hours on Sat. or Sun. – once every one or two month(s). Additionally, if a large enough group was willing to participate, the Troop would host its own service event or fundraiser. I have served as SPL in the past – a position I held for a full year. Furthermore, I have also held the positions of Patrol Leader and Assistant Patrol Leader. I'm passionate about helping the Troop become more involved in service – a more "service oriented" Troop would benefit every Scout (especially those of you working on Life and below)! Please vote for me!
